2 · Code changes the AI made (both validated on out-of-sample)

breakout_buffer (0 → 0.45)
The close must clear the prior range by 0.45 × range before a breakout counts — filters marginal/false breakouts. Side effect: re-enabled both directions (it removes the false short breakouts that used to fail).
cooldown_bars (0 → 4)
After a losing trade, sit out 4 bars before a new entry — curbs whipsaw / revenge re-entries in chop.

6 · Read & next steps

• The in-sample PF of ~2.5 every fold with an out-of-sample PF that collapses = textbook overfitting.
• The strategy is low-frequency (~3 trades / 1,000 bars), so each test window has very few trades — small samples are noisy and the single split got lucky.
• Before trusting any version of this: get more data spanning different market regimes (this is one ~2.5-month, mostly-uptrending ES stretch), then re-run walk-forward. A strategy that survives walk-forward across regimes is worth trading; this one, as-is, is not.
• The two code ideas (breakout buffer, post-loss cooldown) are sound in principle — they just couldn't rescue a thin, regime-specific edge on this data.
