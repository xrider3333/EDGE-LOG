How a backtest runs · start → finish
What each stage means (plain English)

Almost every upgrade below lives in just two spots — Stage 4 (how you pick the winner) and Stage 5 (how you try to break it). EDGELOG already owns Stages 1, 2, 6, 7 and most of Stage 5.

3 · Search — run the strategy hundreds of times with different knob settings ("params"). Grid = try every combo; auto/Bayesian = a smart search that hunts the good region. Both live.
4 · Pick the winner — today EDGELOG grabs the single highest-profit setting. PDP plateau pick = choose the setting sitting in a broad hill of good neighbors, not a lone lucky spike. Ensemble = don't crown one at all — blend the best several so no single fluke decides it. Both just change which row of the results you trust.
5 · Stress the winner — already the strongest stage: walk-forward, Monte-Carlo drawdown, Deflated Sharpe (haircut the score for how many settings you tried), neighborhood, NQ↔ES transfer. The three planned adds drop into the same gate list — adversarial ("has the market changed character since I fit?"), causal ("did my rule make the money, or was I just long in an up year?"), conformal ("report a range, require even the low end positive").
